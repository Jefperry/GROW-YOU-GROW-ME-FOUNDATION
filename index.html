02   The Inspiration
The Why

A continent burdened, a generation determined to rise.

More than ten years after their expected achievement date, the Millennium Development Goals remain painfully unmet across sub‑Saharan Africa. The failure is most severe in education: millions of Africans have never had access to quality schooling, and a great many of the educated have not received an education that is truly holistic.

Cameroon is no exception. In each region there is an inch of this reflection — schooling has become financially dependent and costly, and in the crisis‑stricken rural settings, it has barely taken rise. The few schools that exist cannot effectively do their work, and parents cannot provide the necessary finances to carry their children through.

“Education is the best gift you can give to man — and it is, indeed, the greatest weapon that can be used to change the world.”

Driven by this force and enthusiasm, the inception of Grow You Grow Me becomes worthwhile. The name is not reciprocal — it is multiplicative. If the foundation grows one person, that person is asked to grow two others. We believe that by growing you, we ourselves grow.

Mission Statement

In the spirit of service to humanity and love for education — the greatest weapon to effectuate world change — we seek to provide free, quality and holistic education to young Cameroonians, from secondary through to high school level, inculcating the spirit of service to one another, and the capacity to fend for themselves.

03   What We Do
Aims & Objectives

Four commitments, one shared future.

In its first years, the foundation is focused on four distinct but interconnected pillars — each designed to remove the barriers that keep young Cameroonians from the education they deserve.

01

Scholarships for thousands

We subsidize the education of deserving Cameroonians — covering a meaningful percentage of school fees so that financial hardship is never the reason a bright mind is left behind.

02

School materials for villages

At the start of every academic year, we reach into villages with bags, books, pens and the didactic materials pupils need — practical tools placed directly into the hands of those who need them most.

03

Schools we build ourselves

Our principal aim is to build our own schools in vulnerable regions — places where quality, holistic education will be offered for completely no fees. Fees are asked only if a student repeats a class, so that serendipity itself becomes a reward for resilience.

04

Serviceable, self‑reliant citizens

We train students to be of service — to extend a hand to the next person, to become vision creators and self‑employers, and to step into the professional world with both competence and conscience.

04   Our Story
The Inception

Born from lived experience, raised on hope.

The RootsBefore 2026

Walking the path ourselves

Those of us who were sponsored through SAHECO, or passed through her halls, came to know the struggles of education intimately — from crisis and displacement, from deadbeat parents, from the very institutions meant to lift us up.

“Beyond Death” — a launch, a spark

On the 4th of April 2026, in Bamenda Old Town, the foundation was first incepted during the launch of the novel Beyond Death by Formbuwing Dieudonne — in the presence of SHESANS, NACDA, the Fon of Awing, and other advocates for education.

We are young — but we are full of dreams, hopes and the willing spirit to drive change into society. Every scholarship issued, every schoolbag handed over, every stone laid for a new classroom is a step toward that future.
